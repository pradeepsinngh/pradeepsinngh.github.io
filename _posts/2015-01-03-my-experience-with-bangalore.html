As I sit and share my experience about one of my most favorite cities in the world, I'm infused with memories. Bangalore happened to me when i am little less than 21. I came here 10 days back to work on a Research Project at Ramam Research Institute .

Since then its been a great experinece, professionally as well as personally.

So, my first impression of Bangalore is something which is very different from other citites for variety of reasons. (Oh NO , It has nothing to do with Silicon Valley of India or IT capital of India).

Rather, its has more to do with the Humans (Humans of Bengaluru). On my very first day, (Yes, 24th Dec 2014) I met these 2 beautiful people, who were more than happy to go out of there way and help me. So here are these 2 small stories.

A Old Lady (45 - 50 Years) :-

She was coming back from Pune, where her daughter is living with her husband. We had a small conversation (5 Mins) during Train journey but never thought that she will be so motherly in Nature.

I reached SBC at 9:05 AM and was looking for a Cab/ Auto to commute to the my hostel (Where i was supposed to stay/ And I am currently staying at the same place- Malleswaram).

She came to me :- Where do you want to go? "Malleswaram," I said.
She said (Smiling) :- You better take Bus, its hardly 10-15 Mins.
"No, Mam. It's Ohk. I'll Manage, " I replied.
She said, "come I will take you to the Bus Stop".

Then, we walked for 2 mins and reached Majestic Bus Stop.

P.S. :- The moment we reached Majestic, I was like what the hell -- I had no idea, what was that. So many buses in so less space. Till, then i didn't had any idea about Bangalore Traffic and congestion problem particulary near KBS/Majestic Area.

She took me to the platform no 22 (In Bangalore, they have platform for Buses also. Strange. Same was with me). And said, you can take any bus from here.

By the time next bus came, she was standing just next to me. Just to make sure, that i take correct bus. How amazing she was. :)

Next Bus came, And i got into it and she waived her hands to say Bye and so did I.

Within next 12-15 Mins, I was in Malleswaram. then I realized, I didn't even said Thanks to her (I felt bad... and I should).

So, this was my first interaction with these amazing Humans of Bangalore.

Checked in the Hostel, completed all the formalities. And, was ready to report to the place where i was supposed to work for next couple of months (Yes, RRI).
