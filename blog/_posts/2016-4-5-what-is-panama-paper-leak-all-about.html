The Panama Papers is an international Project carried out by many media organizations across the world over last one year.

1.) What is Panama leak all about?
Panama Papers (international collaborative project) have leaked the significant documents (approximately 11.5 million documents totaling 2.6 terabytes of data) about Mossack Fonseca firm (an off shore law firm) in Panama (A country in Central America) which has helped investors around the world to evade tax, launder money, among others.

2.) What is Panama Papers all about?
The Panama Papers is the largest international cooperation of its kind in the world. In the last one year, around 400 journalists from more than 100 media organizations in over 80 countries have taken part in researching the documents (leaked by anonymous source).

Journalists from media organizations like BBC, Guardian, Süddeutsche Zeitung, and The Indian Express among others have also worked on the project.

The leak is considered to be the largest in the Journalism world.

The Papers have revealed names of thousands of people from across the world who evaded the tax and laundered money by taking help from Mossack Fonseca.



3.) What is an offshore law firm? What it does..?
An offshore law firm is a firm that sells anonymous offshore companies around the world.

These shell companies allow their promoters to hide their business dealings. They have dozens of offices all over the world.

Their job is to found, sold and managed thousands of companies and thus making huge profit and helping others to make their black money into white/ evaded taxes.

A shell corporation is a corporation without active business operations or significant assets. Shell corporations are not necessarily illegal or illegitimate, as they often serve an important for potential startups. Additionally, shell corporations can act as a tax avoidance for legitimate businesses. Source

The law firm helped clients secretly create a maze of accounts to make their money untraceable & thus converting black into white or evading taxes etc. These companies makes it easy for others to found companies, by selling its shell companies in cities across the world.

They provide loads of facilities & at a very cheaper rate, from buying an anonymous company for as little as $1,000 (nearly Rs 66,290), to providing a sham (fake) director, to holding board meetings. And if the clients desire, it conceals the company's true shareholder. In short, the company is into the business of withholding the true identity of the offshore company's owner.

4.) What is Mossack Fonseca?
A law firm based out in Panama which sells its shell companies around the world.

ICIJ likes to describe them as gatekeeper to the secrets of its clients, even those who are crooks, mafioso, drug dealers, corrupt politicians and tax evaders. Mosack Foneca is one of world’s five biggest company when it comes to selling offshore companies.

5.) How leak took place?
An anonymous source contacted a German newspaper Süddeutsche Zeitung (SZ) over a year ago period and submitted encrypted internal documents from Mossack Fonseca datebase.

6.) What's the leaked data all about?
The data leaked is mainly comprises of emails, pdf files, photo files, and excerpts of an internal Mossack Fonsecadatabase.

It reveals information on how an overseas industry led by major banks, legal firms, and asset management companies secretly manages the assets of the world's rich and famous personalities from politicians, FIFA officials, fraudsters, drug smugglers, to celebrities and professional athletes.

It provided a clear picture of how Mossack Fonseca routinely accepts investments/ money from world's rich and famous people to engage in business activities that potentially violate laws of many countries, besides helping and abetting tax evasion and money laundering.

7.) How big is this leak?
The scale of this leak is the biggest of all time leaks that world has seen in past.

It includes approximately 11.5 million documents, totaling 2.6 terabytes (TB) of data which is higher than the combined total of the Wikileaks/ Cablegate, Offshore Leaks, Lux Leaks, and Swiss Leaks.

No doubt, it’s the mother of all leaks.

    10.) So, is this a fraud case? (from Indian perspective)
    Yes, it is.
    But, it all depends when they have invested (in which year) & also whether they have informed the authorities about their assets/ earnings in abroad in past or not.

    According to the law if anyone has invested before 2004 is in violation of the law. And, as per RBI, those setting up or buying companies before 2013 are in‘technical’ violation of the act.

    If individuals have kept the government informed of their investments/ earnings abroad through these companies, then there is a weak case.

    But if the authorities were not informed of the assets held abroad then there are various acts that are triggered, such as the money laundering act and FEMA, among others & they should/ will be prosecuted.
