Internet is big, very big indeed. As a result there's a lot of knowledge & content everywhere, but it's really hard to find quality stuff that interests you.

Over the last many years I have started following many blogs reguralry & now they have become a part of my life. I thought of maintaing a list of such content.

This is an eclectic set of content that I read and watch regularly or have been immensely moved or educated by.
The list is a constant work in progress .

If you don't have so much time (Or, you are busy) to read/watch such content. I will strongly recommened you to follow Quora, Reddit, Hacker News.

I am also found here: Hacker News · Reddit · Quora

Stimulating Reads

Aggregations of interesting and informative pieces of writing.

  1. Hacker News - The one stop source for all the latest news and information in technology & startups.
  2. Quora - Quora is an Q & A website where questions are asked, answered, edited and organized by its community of users. Arguably the best place to get insgihts on anything Or everything.
  3. Reddit - The link sharing site(actually, it's much more!). The front page of Interent. Source of news/resources/links for possibly everything on this palnet.
  4. Paul Graham's Essays - Venture capitalist and founder of Y Combinator writes the simplest yet most captivating essays about how to live life, startups, knowledge, and technology.
  5. Sam Altman's Blog - Venture capitalist and lecturer of the Stanford course on "How To Start a Startup" speaks about startups, technology and entrepreneurship.
  6. Essays and Speeches by Charlie Munger - The long time friend and advisor to Warren Buffet speaks and writes of economics, finance, psychology, and overall worldly wisdom. I haven't read all of them, but I wish finish it very soon.
  7. Richard Muller - Prof Physics, UC Berkeley, author "Energy for Future Presidents" & Co-founder of Berkeley Earth. His insights on Physcis are worth reading.
  8. Farnam Street - A blog that helps you go to bed smarter than you woke up. It speaks of philosophy, wisdom, happiness, knowledge and meaning.
  9. What are the chances of your coming into being?

Comics & Humor

One stop source for humor when you can't watch a video.

  1. xkcd - Randall Munroe's comic strip about technology, science, mathematics and relationships. Possibly read by every technologist, scientist, mathematician, and more.
  2. SMBC - Saturday Morning Breakfast Cereal (SMBC) is a comic strip that features atheism, romance, science, research and the meaning of life.
  3. The Oatmeal - Home to famous Nikola Tesla and Mantis Shrimp comics, The Oatmeal, created in 2009 by Matthew Inman, covers an eclectic range of topics. Inman says it typically takes 7-8 hours of research to produce one comic.
  4. PhD Comics - Piled Higher and Deeper, is a comic strip written by Caltech instructor Jorge Cham that follows graduate students' lives and stories.

Stirring Videos

A list of stuff that's inspiring, informative, and exceptionally helpful in moulding your worldview.

  1. Noam Chomsky's Speeches - While Chomsky may be slightly monotonous, he is almost indomitable in debate on account of the depth of his knowledge & breath of his acumen.
  2. You and Your Research - An talk given by Dr. Richard Hamming at the Morris Research and Engineering Center on March 7, 1986. Must watch for every Engineer & Researcher.
  3. Harvard Innovation Lab - Talks on new and innovative initiative fostering team-based and entrepreneurial activities.
  4. Oxford Union - It's the world's most prestigious debating society, with an unparalleled reputation for bringing international guests and speakers to Oxford for one-on-one debate. Watch it! Two of my all time favourite speeches are this & this.
  5. Harvard Innovation Lab - Talks on new and innovative initiative fostering team-based and entrepreneurial activities.
  6. INK Talks - An India-focused extension of TED.
  7. TED Talks - Under twenty minute talks about 'Ideas worth Spreading' ranging in dozens of topics. Features innovation and inspiration.
