Home

This is an eclectic set of content that I read and watch regularly or have been immensely moved or educated by. The list is a constant work in progress .

Stimulating Reads

Aggregations of interesting and informative pieces of writing.
  1. Richard Muller - Prof Physics, UC Berkeley, author "Energy for Future Presidents" & Co-founder of Berkeley Earth. His insights on Physcis are worth reading.
  2. Farnam Street - A blog that helps you go to bed smarter than you woke up. It speaks of philosophy, wisdom, happiness, knowledge and meaning.

Comics & Humor

One stop source for humor when you can't watch a video.
  1. xkcd- Randall Munroe's comic strip about technology, science, mathematics and relationships. Possibly read by every technologist, scientist, mathematician, and more.
  2. SMBC- Saturday Morning Breakfast Cereal (SMBC) is a comic strip that features atheism, romance, science, research and the meaning of life.
  3. The Oatmeal-Home to famous Nikola Tesla and Mantis Shrimp comics, The Oatmeal, created in 2009 by Matthew Inman, covers an eclectic range of topics. Inman says it typically takes 7-8 hours of research to produce one comic.
  4. PhD Comics-PhD, or Piled Higher and Deeper, is a comic strip written by Caltech instructor Jorge Cham that follows graduate students' lives and stories.

Stirring Videos

A list of stuff that's inspiring, informative, and exceptionally helpful in moulding your worldview.
  1. Noam Chomsky's Speeches- While Chomsky may be slightly monotonous, he is almost indomitable in debate on account of the depth of his knowledge and breath of his acumen.
  2. You and Your Research- An talk by Dr. Richard Hamming at the Morris Research and Engineering Center on March 7, 1986. Must watch for every Engineer & Researcher.
  3. Harvard Innovation Lab- Talks on new and innovative initiative fostering team-based and entrepreneurial activities.
  4. INK Talks- An India-focused extension of TED.
  5. TED Talks- Under twenty minute talks about 'Ideas worth Spreading' ranging in dozens of topics. Features innovation and inspiration.
  6. Noam Chomsky's Speeches- While Chomsky may be slightly monotonous, he is almost indomitable in debate on account of the depth of his knowledge and breath of his acumen.
